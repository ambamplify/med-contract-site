Our mission

We built this because internal medicine and hospitalist physicians deserve better.

Emergency medicine is one of the most demanding specialties in medicine. The contracts you sign should reflect that — and most don't.

Why MedContractIntel™ exists

Emergency medicine physicians generate enormous value for hospitals and staffing companies — and for decades, that value has been systematically underpriced. The average internal medicine and hospitalist physicians working for a national staffing company earns $21/wRVU below the market median. At 6,000 wRVUs per year, that's $120,000 in annual income left on the table — not because physicians aren't worth more, but because most don't have the data to know what "more" looks like.

Contracts are written by attorneys and administrators who negotiate them every day. Most physicians negotiate once every few years — and do it blind, without benchmarks, without a clause-by-clause breakdown, and without knowing which terms are standard and which are red flags.

MedContractIntel levels that playing field. We give emergency physicians the same quality of analysis that a seasoned healthcare attorney would provide — delivered in under 5 minutes, for $97.

To make data-driven contract analysis accessible to every internal medicine and hospitalist physician — from PGY-4s signing their first attending contract to seasoned attendings renegotiating at renewal — so that no internal medicine and hospitalist physicians signs a contract without knowing exactly what it's worth and what to change.

What this tool is — and isn't

MedContractIntel provides educational and data-driven financial analysis. It identifies compensation gaps, contract risks, and negotiation priorities based on current market benchmarks. It is not legal advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship. For contracts with high-risk clauses — especially complex non-compete agreements or disputed termination terms — we recommend consulting a licensed healthcare attorney. Our reports include referrals to AHLA member attorneys in your state when appropriate.
