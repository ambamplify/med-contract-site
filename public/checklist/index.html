physician contract Red Flag Checklist

15 provisions most likely to cost you money. Check each one before you sign any internal medicine and hospitalist employment contract. No email required.

  • 1
    High Risk

    RVU multiplier below 25th percentile for your employer type

    The single biggest source of physician underpayment. Ranges vary by specialty and employer type (MGMA + AMGA + SHM + Doximity 2025). Confirm your contract rate against the IM_DATA_2026 benchmark for your specialty and employer type. If your rate is below the 25th percentile, flag it. Always compare against IM & hospitalist-specific benchmarks — not general "physician" data.

  • 2
    High Risk

    Non-compete radius greater than 15 miles

    In dense metro areas, a 25–30 mile non-compete can eliminate every hospital and affiliated outpatient practice in your market. Industry standard for IM & hospitalist is 10–15 miles, often less in hospital-system-affiliate contracts. Anything larger requires aggressive negotiation or legal review.

  • 3
    High Risk

    Non-compete duration longer than 1 year

    2-year non-competes are common in staffing company contracts but rarely enforceable in full. Many states cap enforceability at 1 year. Duration should be negotiated down before signing.

  • 4
    High Risk

    Physician responsible for tail malpractice coverage

    Tail coverage can cost $20,000–$60,000+ for an internal medicine and hospitalist physicians. If the contract places this obligation on you after termination, negotiate for the employer to cover it or factor the cost into your compensation ask.

  • 5
    High Risk

    Termination without cause notice shorter than 90 days

    30-day termination clauses are common in staffing company contracts and leave you dangerously exposed. 90 days is the minimum acceptable notice for a primary job. 180 days is reasonable to request.

  • 6
    High Risk

    APC supervision haircut greater than 25%

    Many contracts reduce your effective RVU rate when you supervise APCs (PAs/NPs). A haircut over 25% of your supervised APC production is financially punitive. Calculate your effective hourly rate with and without APC supervision days.

  • 7
    Moderate Risk

    Unilateral right to modify compensation formula

    Language allowing the employer to change your RVU conversion factor, bonus thresholds, or compensation model with 30-day notice (or less) eliminates your income predictability. Require mutual written agreement for material changes.

  • 8
    Moderate Risk

    Billing gag clause (limits discussion of billing practices)

    Some contracts prohibit physicians from discussing billing codes, collections rates, or payor mix with colleagues or patients. These clauses have legal exposure under state and federal law and should be removed.

  • 9
    Moderate Risk

    Exclusive services clause (locks you to employer exclusively)

    If the contract prohibits moonlighting or outside clinical work entirely, it limits your income flexibility and leverage. Negotiate for a carve-out for pre-approved outside work, locums, or academic activities.

  • 10
    Moderate Risk

    No COLA (cost of living adjustment) provision in multi-year contracts

    A fixed RVU multiplier in a 3-year contract with no inflation adjustment is a real pay cut every year. Request annual COLA tied to CPI or a fixed 2–3% step increase.

  • 11
    Moderate Risk

    Mandatory arbitration with employer-selected arbitrator

    Arbitration clauses are common, but employer-selected arbitrators create a structural conflict of interest. Require a neutral arbitration service (AAA or JAMS) or mutual selection of arbitrator.

  • 12
    Moderate Risk

    Restrictive assignment of benefits (locks you out of insurance networks post-termination)

    Some contracts assign your provider network participation to the employer. After termination, you may need to re-credentialize with every payor from scratch. This can delay your next position by months.

  • 13
    Moderate Risk

    No sign-on bonus or relocation assistance in rural/underserved settings

    If you're accepting a rural or underserved position, sign-on bonuses of $20,000–$50,000 and relocation assistance are standard. Absence of these in high-need markets is a negotiating opportunity.

  • 14
    Moderate Risk

    Vague or undefined "productivity" metrics that can be changed unilaterally

    If your bonus or at-risk compensation ties to "productivity" without defining the specific metric, threshold, and measurement period, you have no protection against goal-post moving. Require specificity in writing.

  • 15
    Moderate Risk

    No clear definition of "qualified RVUs" (total vs. work RVUs)

    Your contract must specify work RVUs (wRVUs), not total RVUs. Total RVUs include practice expense and malpractice components that inflate the number without reflecting physician effort. A $30/total RVU contract may be worth $20/wRVU in reality.
